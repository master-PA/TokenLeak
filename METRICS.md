# METRICS

## North Star Metric
**Qualified Leads Generated (per week)**
*Why:* TokenLeak is not a standalone SaaS product designed for daily active engagement. It is a B2B lead-generation tool for Credex. A "Qualified Lead" is defined as an email captured from an audit that identified >$500/mo in savings. Tracking visitors, total audits, or "DAU" is vanity; if the tool is popular but fails to generate high-intent pipeline for Credex, it is a failure. Optimizing for Qualified Leads forces us to attract the *right* persona (companies with significant API spend), not just indie hackers looking for $10/mo savings.

## 3 Input Metrics (Drivers)
1. **Audits Completed per Visitor (Conversion Rate):** Measures the effectiveness of the landing page copy and the low-friction input form.
2. **Email Capture Rate (Lead Conversion):** The percentage of completed audits that result in an email submission. This measures the perceived value of the audit results (does the user trust the math enough to give us their email?).
3. **K-Factor (Viral Coefficient from Share Links):** The number of new, unique visitors generated by each shared audit link. This drives the top of the funnel organically.

## What to Instrument First
Before launching, I would instrument the funnel drops using PostHog or Amplitude:
1. `page_view` (Landing Page)
2. `audit_started` (First input field changed)
3. `audit_completed` (Results generated)
4. `share_link_created` (User clicked share)
5. `lead_captured` (Email submitted, with a property for `savings_amount`)

## The Pivot Trigger
**If Email Capture Rate < 2% after 1,000 completed audits.**
If 1,000 people take the time to input their stack and see their savings, but fewer than 20 give us their email, it means one of two things:
1. The tool is attracting the wrong audience (people with $0 savings who have no reason to talk to Credex).
2. The audit results are not perceived as valuable or defensible enough to warrant a consultation.
At this point, we must pivot the "Aha!" moment—perhaps by gating the most valuable part of the analysis, or by shifting the target audience to larger enterprises where the savings are too big to ignore.
